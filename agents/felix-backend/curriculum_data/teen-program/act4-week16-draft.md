## Section 11 — Structural Notes

**Week 16 is two events, not one.** The WIN ceremony follows the standard WIN week protocol exactly — verification, scoreboard, WIN COUNT record. The Graduation follows after, and it is a distinct ceremony with its own purpose. Do not collapse them. The WIN ceremony closes Act 4; the Graduation closes the program.

**WIN COUNT and ceremony are unconditional.** Even if Beat 1 or Beat 2 verification is partial: the scoreboard is shown, the Output Format delta is read aloud, WIN COUNT = 1 (Act 4) is recorded, total WIN COUNT = 4/4 is shown. The verification result is data — named, noted, carried into the student's own maintenance practice. It does not withhold the ceremony or the Graduation.

**The Graduation Artifact must be pre-assembled.** Do not have students assemble their own system prompt during the ceremony from scratch. Each line corresponds to one Act. The cadence should be felt. Read it as written. Paraphrasing flattens the impact.

**Scoreboards are individual, not comparative.** In the 2-student pilot, show each student's WIN COUNT ledger, trajectory snapshot, and Graduation Artifact individually, one at a time. The Graduation ceremony is each student's own story — not a comparison.
