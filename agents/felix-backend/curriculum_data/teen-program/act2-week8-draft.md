## Section 2 — What the Student Brings In

The student enters Week 8 with:

1. **Their identity card from Act 1** — unchanged since Week 2 (with possible 5-minute quick-patch from Week 6 for Type B students).
2. **Their revised Workflow from the async submission** — includes the original linear arc steps from Week 6 PLUS the new non-linear arc conditional for Post 3's callback. Double-gap students also have a revised Step 3 with named-reference precision.
3. **A named gap from Week 7** — the student knows exactly what they added to their Workflow and why. They are not guessing.
4. **A planted expectation from Week 7** — *"Same STRETCH task, one more run. Then we look at everything — four weeks of scores, every dimension. That's the WIN ceremony."* Students know the structure. The ceremony is not a surprise.

**What the student expects:**

Students arrive expecting two things: a verification run and a scoreboard. The verification run creates mild anticipation — they wrote a fix, they want to see if it worked. The scoreboard creates a different kind of anticipation — they have not seen all their scores together before. Week 7 scores were stored but not shared in-session. The full picture is new.

The session's job is to give both things clearly and without rush. Beat 3 should feel like a close, not like a test.

## Section 3 — Beat 1 — Verification Run 1 (Linear Arc, Week 5 Task Repeat)

**Trigger:** Pre-session check complete. Student has a revised Workflow loaded. Verification Run 1 uses the Week 5 series task prompt — the original failing task.

**What the facilitator says:**

*[Pause.]*

*"Same prompt, same agent. Let's see if the fix you added to handle the STRETCH task broke anything on the one that was already working."*

---

**Beat 1 task card (exact screen text):**

> **Your task: Re-run the Week 5 series**
>
> Your agent is the revised version — identity card plus your updated Workflow from the async assignment.
>
> **Ask your agent this exact question:**
>
> *"I need a 3-post series for my creator brand. Each post needs a hook, a main point, and a call to action. Post 2 should build on and name something specific from Post 1. Post 3 should close the arc and reference where the series started."*
>
> **After you get the output:**
>
> **Step 1.** Read Post 2's first sentence. Does it name something specific from Post 1 — a specific claim, phrase, or question — or does it say "in this series" / "following on from Part 1"?
>
> **Step 2.** Compare to your Week 5 output. Same task. Same question. Did Post 2 name Post 1 this time?
>
> **Step 3.** Note your Post 2 first sentence. Hit **Submit**.

---

**Facilitator notes — Beat 1:**

- **Beat 1 is low-stakes by design.** The linear arc task is the one the Workflow was already built for in Week 6. The purpose is a two-part check: (1) confirm the linear arc still works; (2) confirm the new non-linear conditional didn't introduce a conflict that breaks the linear case.
- **If Post 2's first sentence NAMES Post 1 specifically:** The fix didn't break the linear case. Note the result. Move to Beat 2.
- **If Post 2's first sentence says "in this series" or "following on from":** The revision introduced a regression — either the new conditional is too broad and overrode the Step 3 instruction, or the double-gap student's Step 3 rewrite introduced ambiguity. Note this without comment. The WIN ceremony still runs. Flag the issue for the Section 10 capture.
- **Single run only.** Beat 1 is a quick confirmation, not a full diagnostic. One run is sufficient. If the student wants to run twice, allow it — but only the first run is scored.

## Section 5 — Beat 3 — WIN Ceremony

**Trigger:** Beat 1 and Beat 2 complete. Results noted. Now the scoreboard.

**What makes this a ceremony:**

The scoreboard is the physical artifact of Act 2. The student has never seen all their scores together — Week 5, Week 6, Week 7, and now Week 8. The facilitator does not just display the board. The facilitator reads the Series coherence delta out loud before the board is shown. The student hears the number before they see it.

For 15-17 year olds, hearing a number before seeing it creates a beat of anticipation. That beat is the ceremony. It does not need to be long. It needs to be deliberate.

---

**Facilitator script — WIN ceremony opening:**

*[Beat 2 is done. Before touching the screen: turn to the student.]*

*"Before I show you the scoreboard — I'm going to read you one number."*

*[Pause.]*

*[Pause.]*

*[Read the Series coherence delta from your facilitator notes: Week 8 actual minus Week 5 baseline.]*

*"Your Series coherence moved [delta] points since Week 5."*

*[Pause. Let them sit with the number.]*

*"Now let's look at everything."*

*[Display the scoreboard.]*

## Section 8 — Scoring — Week 8

**Scoring protocol:**
1. Score Beat 1 (linear arc run) on all six dimensions using the standard Week 5–7 rubric. Series coherence uses the linear arc standard (Post 2 names Post 1 in first sentence).
2. Score Beat 2 (STRETCH run) on Series coherence only using the Week 7 non-linear arc addendum. The other five dimensions are assumed stable — use Beat 1 scores for Consistency, Specificity, Local grounding, Questioning behavior, and Claim integrity.
3. Official Week 8 Series coherence score = lower of Beat 1 Series coherence score and Beat 2 Series coherence score.
4. Official Week 8 total = Beat 1 scores for five dimensions + official Series coherence score.
5. Compare Week 8 official total to Week 5 baseline. Calculate delta per dimension for the scoreboard.

**Expected score movement (Week 8 vs Week 5):**

| Dimension | Expected movement | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Consistency | Stable ± 0–5 | Identity card unchanged since Act 1 |
| Specificity | Stable ± 0–5 | Identity card unchanged |
| Local grounding | Stable | Unchanged throughout Act 2 |
| Questioning behavior | Stable | Behavior instructions unchanged |
| Claim integrity | Stable | Arc type doesn't affect claim patterns |
| **Series coherence** | **+10 to +20 (the Act 2 WIN)** | Workflow fix closes the named gap; revised conditional handles both arc types |

**Research and Output Format dimensions:** Scored as 0 or "N/A — not yet built." Record on the scoreboard as-is. Do not adjust or inflate. Framing note in Section 5 handles the student reaction.

**No new scoring components in Week 8.** Act 2's instrument is complete at 6 dimensions × 20 = 0–120.

## Section 10 — Structural Notes

**WIN week discipline — no new content.** If students ask what Act 3 covers in detail: give the one-sentence bridge ("where does your agent get its facts?") and no more. Do not introduce Research Grounding mechanics in Week 8. Week 9 opens with a FAIL first — the revelation that the agent makes things up. That revelation lands harder when students don't know it's coming.

**WIN COUNT and ceremony are unconditional (Ruling 1, Week 7 Section 13).** The WIN ceremony is not a quality gate. Even if Beat 1 or Beat 2 verification shows a partial or failed result: the scoreboard is shown, the Series coherence delta is read aloud, WIN COUNT = 1 is recorded. The verification result is one more data point — named, noted, carried into Act 3. It does not withhold the ceremony.

**Double-gap students (Week 6 PARTIAL).** Beat 1 provides an additional check: the Step 3 precision fix. If Beat 1 Post 2 names Post 1 specifically, the Step 3 revision held. If Beat 1 Post 2 is still vague, the double-gap student's Step 3 rewrite was insufficient. Note the result. The WIN ceremony proceeds regardless.

**Scoreboard is individual, not comparative.** In a 2-student pilot, the temptation is to show both scoreboards side by side. Resist this. Each student's board is their record of their own trajectory. Competitive framing undermines the WIN ceremony's purpose, which is to close Act 2 with a clear personal account.
