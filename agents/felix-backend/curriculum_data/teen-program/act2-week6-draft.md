## Section 4 — Zara's Workflow Section (Screen 1 — Beat 1 Reading Material)

*This is the exact text students see on Screen 1 during Beat 1. It is Zara's Workflow section only — part of her full system prompt. Students do NOT see Zara's Persona, Scope, or Behavior sections this week. Display in plain, readable format with the "Workflow" header clearly visible — students use this same header when building their version.*

---

**What students see on Screen 1:**

---

**Workflow**

When you ask me to create a 3-post series:

- **Step 1 — Map the arc before writing anything.** In one sentence each, name what Post 1 will open (the question or promise), what Post 2 will deliver (the answer or payoff), and what Post 3 will close (the resolution). State this plan before writing the first post.
- **Step 2 — Write Post 1.** Post 1 must end with a specific, named promise or open question — something concrete enough that a reader knows exactly what to expect in Post 2. The CTA must point forward: "Part 2 drops [day]" or "Follow for Part 2 — I'm sharing [X] next."
- **Step 3 — Write Post 2.** The first sentence of Post 2 must name something specific from Post 1 by direct reference — for example: "In Part 1, I said [X] — here's what I found" or "In my last post I promised [specific thing] — here it is." Do not open Post 2 with new information or a fresh hook. Post 2 opens with the callback. The rest of Post 2 delivers on what Post 1 promised.
- **Step 4 — Write Post 3.** Post 3 must explicitly close what Post 1 opened. Name the series ("Final post in this series" or "If you've been following from Part 1") and bring the arc to a resolution. The CTA invites engagement on the completed series, not on a new topic.
- **Step 5 — Self-check before delivering.** Read Post 2's first sentence: does it name something specific from Post 1? Read Post 3's opening: does it acknowledge the series and close what Post 1 opened? If either fails the check, revise before sending.

---

**Facilitator notes — Beat 2 circulation:**

Circulate and check each student's draft before giving the GO. The facilitator GO is a hard gate — do not let students run the Beat 3 test on a Workflow that will fail for the wrong reasons. A structurally broken Workflow means the test result won't teach anything.

**The GO checklist (verbal — facilitator use only, not shown to students):**

1. Does the Workflow section have a header ("Workflow")? If no: "Add the header — it needs to be a named section."
2. Does it have numbered or bulleted steps? If no: "Rewrite this as steps. Step 1, Step 2..."
3. Does Step 3 (or equivalent) say to NAME something specific from Post 1 in Post 2's first sentence? "Build on Post 1" is too vague. The instruction must specify naming: "say what Post 1 established," "reference the specific thing you promised in Post 1," "open Post 2 with a callback to what Post 1 set up."
4. Does the section have 3–5 steps? Too few (1–2) = probably a description, not a workflow. Too many (6+) = risk of the agent ignoring later steps.

**GO blockers:**
- Step 3 equivalent is missing entirely → ask student to add it
- Section is prose (not steps) → ask student to rewrite as bullets
- Steps describe post CONTENT, not PROCESS ORDER → see Section 6 language

**Not GO blockers:**
- Steps use different language than the template — expected and fine
- Student added a Step 0 (ask clarifying questions before starting) — excellent, give GO
- Step 5 (self-check) is missing — preferred but not required; mention it, don't block

## Section 6 — The Build Moment — Key Facilitator Language

*Parallel to Week 5's Section 6 (The Failure Moment). This section isolates the highest-leverage facilitator language during Beat 2 — the moments where the build most commonly goes wrong and the exact correction language that keeps it on track.*

---

**The most common build failure — describing content instead of specifying process:**

*What students write:*

> "Post 1 should have a strong hook. Post 2 should develop the idea. Post 3 should wrap up."

*What's wrong:* This describes WHAT each post should contain, not WHEN the agent writes it or what it carries forward. The agent already knows what a hook is. It still doesn't know to write an arc map before Post 1, or to name something specific from Post 1 in Post 2's first sentence.

*What the facilitator says — verbatim:*

"Read this to me as if you're the agent getting these instructions. You've just received this. What do you do first?"

*[Student reads it aloud and realizes there's no first step.]*

"Right. Your agent doesn't know whether to write Post 1 first or Post 2 first. And it doesn't know that Post 2 has to NAME something from Post 1 — not just 'develop it.' Let's rewrite Step 3. Not 'Post 2 develops the idea.' Instead: 'When writing Post 2, the first sentence must name what Post 1 established — for example: In Part 1, I said [X] — here's what I found.' Try that."

---

**The naming instruction — the single most important line in the Workflow section:**

The GO check on Step 3 is the most consequential moment in Beat 2. This is the line that produces the Post 2 named callback in Beat 3. If Step 3 says "build on Post 1" without specifying a named reference, the agent will write a connected-feeling Post 2 without the explicit callback — which scores 10, not 20, on Series coherence.

*Accepted Step 3 formulations (give these as examples if a student's Step 3 is too vague):*
- "The first sentence of Post 2 must say: 'In Part 1, I said [specific thing] — here's what happened'" ✓
- "Post 2 must reference something specific that was promised in Post 1, using the words 'In Part 1' or 'last post I promised'" ✓
- "Post 2 builds on what Post 1 said" ✗ — too vague
- "Post 2 continues from Post 1" ✗ — too vague

*If a student asks "why isn't 'builds on' enough?" — answer:*

"Because your agent doesn't know what 'builds on' looks like at the sentence level. Does it mean the same topic? The same voice? Starting with 'following on from earlier'? All of those count as 'builds on.' You need to be specific enough that there's only one way to read the instruction. 'Name something specific from Post 1 in the first sentence of Post 2' — that's one specific thing. Your agent can't misinterpret it."

---

**The process-order distinction — why Step 1 (plan before writing) matters:**

Some students skip the arc-mapping step and jump straight to Post 1 instructions. This works sometimes but fails on complex series — without an explicit arc map, the agent anchors less reliably on what Post 1 promised when it reaches Post 2.

*If a student skips Step 1 — say:*

"When your agent gets to Post 2 and needs to name something from Post 1 — how does it know what to name? It goes back to look at Post 1 and extract something. Sometimes that works. But if Post 1 was vague, or the clarifying questions changed direction — the agent may name the wrong thing or nothing specific. Step 1 — 'plan the arc before writing anything' — gives your agent a reference point before the first word is written. It knows exactly what Post 1 will promise. Then Step 3 names that thing. The chain is complete."

## Section 9 — Scoring Instrument — Week 6

**Purpose:** Score each student's agent output from Beat 3's two runs. Compare against Week 5 baseline. The DELTA is the primary data point — students see movement, not just a new number. Series coherence is the WIN dimension.

**When to score:** After Beat 3 closes. Score both runs per student.

**Scoring protocol:**
1. Score both runs (Run 1 and Run 2) on all six dimensions.
2. Use the **lower** of the two run scores per dimension as the official Week 6 score — same protocol as Week 5.
3. Sum six dimension scores for the official total (0–120).
4. Retrieve each student's Week 5 official scores. Calculate delta per dimension: [Week 6 score] − [Week 5 score].
5. Identify Series coherence delta — this is the primary indicator the Workflow build succeeded.
6. Store Week 6 scores and deltas for Week 8 WIN ceremony. **Scores are NOT shown to students in full this week.** Exception: facilitator may show each student their Series coherence dimension movement only ("Your Series coherence moved from [X] to [Y] — that's the Workflow working") if time allows. Full scorecard is withheld for Week 8.

### Scoring Instrument — Series Content Quality (0–20 per dimension, 0–120 total)

*Same six-dimension instrument as Week 5. Definitions unchanged to allow cross-week delta comparison. Series coherence note updated for Week 6's named-callback standard.*

| Dimension | 0 | 10 | 20 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Consistency** | The two runs sounded like different agents — voice, tone, or niche shifted noticeably between them | The two runs shared the same niche but voice or stance varied | The two runs sounded like the same person in the same niche — same clarifying questions, same voice and stance recognizable across both sets of three posts |
| **Specificity** | Posts generic — applicable to any creator in any niche; no concrete angle | Posts specific to the student's niche but without a distinctive stance | Posts had a concrete angle, a distinctive point of view, and clear niche grounding |
| **Local grounding** | No meaningful MY/SG reference in any post | MY/SG mentioned but content applicable anywhere | Specific, recognizable local references — places, platform behaviors, cultural context |
| **Questioning behavior** | Agent produced posts without asking clarifying questions | Agent asked one clarifying question before producing the series | Agent asked two or more clarifying questions before producing the series |
| **Claim integrity** | Posts included specific unverifiable statistics stated as fact | Mix — some claims qualified, some statistics without source | No unverifiable statistics; claims absent, qualified, or from the student's stated knowledge |
| **Series coherence** | Fully independent posts — Post 2 does not reference Post 1; posts rearrangeable without loss of meaning | Minimal connection — posts share a topic, may be numbered, but removing the numbers each post stands alone; "following on from Part 1" without naming specific content = 10, not 20 | Clear series — Post 2's first sentence names something specific from Post 1 by direct reference ("In Part 1, I said [X]"); Post 3 explicitly closes what Post 1 opened; a viewer who sees only Post 2 knows they missed Post 1 AND what it contained |

**Evaluator agent rubric — Week 6 addendum:**

All six dimension rubrics are identical to Week 5 (see act2-week5-draft.md, Section 8). The following addendum applies to Week 6 Series coherence scoring only:

**Series coherence — Week 6 evaluator note:**
The 20-point threshold requires Post 2's first sentence to contain a NAMED, specific reference to something Post 1 established or promised. Accepted: "In Part 1, I said [specific X] — here it is" / "Last post I promised [specific X] — here's what I found" / "In my last post I opened [specific question] — here's the answer." NOT accepted for 20 points: "Following on from Part 1," "Part 2 of my series," "As I mentioned before," "Continuing from last time." These forms score 10. The test: would a viewer who missed Post 1 know WHAT Post 1 said (20) — or merely that a Post 1 exists (10)?

**Delta calculation:** Retrieve the student's official Week 5 score per dimension. Report: [Week 6 official score] / [Week 5 official score] / [delta]. Series coherence delta is reported first. Positive delta on Series coherence = Workflow working.

**Conflict 2 — Full vs abbreviated Zara Workflow reveal**

*Decision taken:* Zara's full 5-step Workflow section is shown to students in Beat 1. Students read the complete structure before building their own.

*Arbitration question:* Full reveal reduces build time and error, but students who copy closely may produce a Workflow that is nearly identical to Zara's — making Week 6 feel like transcription rather than design. An alternative is to show only Steps 1 and 5 (arc-mapping and self-check) and have students independently write Steps 2–4 before comparing. This preserves more creative challenge at the cost of higher Beat 2 time and more FAIL risk. Given that the PF struggle already happened in Week 5, and the BUILD week's goal is a working Workflow (not novel design), the full reveal seems correct. Seeking confirmation that the full reveal is the right call for BUILD weeks in this program, or whether abbreviated reveal is preferred.

**Conflict 3 — Type B quick-patch scope and session structure**

*Decision taken:* Type B students receive a 5-minute identity card quick-patch at the start of Beat 2, scoped to the single declaration most likely to undermine the Workflow build. Full identity card revision is deferred to Week 7.

*Arbitration question:* If a Type B student's Persona is generic enough that even a correctly-written Workflow still produces generic posts in Beat 3, the Win Moment won't land — the agent will name Post 1 but the content won't be distinctive. ## Section 12 — Act 2 Week 6 Summary

**What happened this week:**

Students entered Week 6 carrying a felt failure, a named gap, and a test they set themselves. This was the highest-anticipation build session in the program — no preamble needed.

Beat 1 delivered the recipe analogy: a Workflow is the recipe for how your agent executes a task, not a description of what it produces. Students read Zara's full Workflow section for the first time — a 5-step execution instruction for series tasks, ending in a self-check that mirrors the test they set in Week 5.

Beat 2 was the build. Students wrote their own Workflow sections in 3–5 steps, specifying execution order for a series task. All scores stored for Week 8's WIN ceremony, where students see the full dimension-by-dimension comparison against their own Act 2 baseline.

## Section 13 — Stella Arbitration Decisions

*Formal rulings on the three design conflicts flagged in Section 11. Recorded 2026-06-27. These rulings supersede the provisional decisions in Section 11 and govern the pilot run.*

---

**Decision 1 — PARTIAL threshold for advancing to Week 7**

**Ruling: PARTIAL = ADVANCE with advisory. No revision gate at Week 6 for PARTIAL. Only FAIL triggers the pre-Week 7 revision gate.**

Rationale: A PARTIAL student has a functioning Workflow — it produces structured series posts, just without the named-callback specificity. Facilitator notes PARTIAL status in Section 10 capture and flags it to the Week 7 facilitator before that session opens; student continues.

**Decision 2 — Full vs abbreviated Zara Workflow reveal**

**Ruling: FULL 5-step reveal stands. Students see the complete Workflow section in Beat 1.**

Rationale: BUILD weeks invert the FAIL-week structure — the PF struggle happened in Week 5, not this week. Beat 1's job is to make the build target concrete, not to manufacture a new struggle. Showing only Steps 1 and 5 would preserve a superficial challenge (guessing Steps 2–4) at the cost of higher Beat 2 error rates and session time. The design challenge in a BUILD week is adapting the form to the student's own voice and niche — not reverse-engineering the structure. Partial reveal adds ambiguity without pedagogical value at this stage. Full reveal confirmed.
