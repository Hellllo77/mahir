*[Pause.]*

*"Same prompt, same agent — now with the Research section. Let's see if the claim comes back."*

---

**Beat 1 task card (exact screen text):**

> **Your task: Re-run the Week 9 fact task**
>
> Your agent is the updated version — identity card, Workflow, and your Research section (with recency filter).
>
> **Ask your agent the same question as Week 9.** (Facilitator provides the exact Week 9 fact task prompt.)
>
> **After you get the output:**
>
> **Step 1.** Look for the specific claim that was wrong or unverifiable in Week 9 — [facilitator notes the specific claim verbatim]. Does it appear again? Or does the output handle that topic differently?
>

>
> **Step 3.** Note your result. Hit **Submit**.

---

**Facilitator notes — Beat 1:**

- **Beat 1 is a regression check.** The primary question is whether the specific Week 9 failure recurs. If it doesn't, the Research section's basic function is confirmed. If it does, the Research section may not be specific enough for this exact claim category — note without alarm; the WIN ceremony still runs.
- **Also check Claim Integrity broadly.** Week 9's original claim was one instance. In the same output, are other specific claims now attributed or qualified? The Research section should improve the output overall, not just suppress the one flagged claim.
- **Single run only.** Beat 1 is a confirmation, not a diagnostic. One run is sufficient.

## Section 8 — Scoring — Week 12

**Scoring protocol:**
1. Score Beat 1 (Week 9 fact task repeat) on all seven dimensions using the Week 9–11 rubric. Research Grounding assessed on: (a) Source Declaration — does the Research section in the system prompt contain specific source-path instructions? (b) Claim Integrity — does the output reflect those instructions (no bare invented claims; personal-opinion flags or verifiable attributions present)?
2. Score Beat 2 (STRETCH task repeat) on Research Grounding only using the recency filter check: does the output contain time-anchor signals on time-sensitive claims? Use Beat 1 scores for the other six dimensions.
3. Official Week 12 Research Grounding score = lower of Beat 1 Research Grounding score and Beat 2 Research Grounding score.
4. Official Week 12 total = Beat 1 scores for six dimensions + official Research Grounding score.
5. Compare Week 12 official total to Week 9 baseline. Calculate delta per dimension for the scoreboard.

**Expected score movement (Week 12 vs Week 9):**

| Dimension | Expected movement | Driver |
|---|---|---|
| Consistency | Stable ± 0–5 | Identity card unchanged since Act 1 |
| Specificity | Stable ± 0–5 | Identity card unchanged |
| Local grounding | Stable | Unchanged |
| Questioning behavior | Stable | Unchanged |
| Claim integrity | +5 to +10 | Research section's uncertainty protocol reduces confident-wrong claims vs Week 9 |
| Series coherence | Stable | Workflow unchanged |
| **Research Grounding** | **+10 to +20 (the Act 3 WIN)** | Research section built (Week 10) + recency filter added (Week 11 async fix). Both sub-components — Source Declaration and Claim Integrity — should now score at 10–20. |

**Output Format:** Scored as 0 or "N/A — not yet built." Record on the scoreboard as-is. Framing in Section 5 and Section 6 handles the student reaction.

## Section 10 — Structural Notes

**WIN week discipline — no new content.** If students ask what Act 4 covers in detail: give the Act 4 bridge script (Section 6) and no more. Do not introduce Output Format mechanics or show how to write Output Format instructions. Week 13 opens with a FAIL first — the optional preview in Section 6 is a visual only, not an explanation. The failure in Week 13 lands harder when students don't know how to fix it yet.

**WIN COUNT and ceremony are unconditional.** Even if Beat 1 or Beat 2 verification is partial or shows the recency filter didn't fully take: the scoreboard is shown, the Research Grounding delta is read aloud, WIN COUNT = 1 is recorded. The verification result is one more data point — named, noted, carried into Act 4. It does not withhold the ceremony.

**Scoreboard is individual, not comparative.** In the 2-student pilot, show each student's board individually. Each board is a record of that student's own trajectory. Competitive framing undermines the WIN ceremony's purpose.

**The Act 3 scoreboard is anchored to Week 9.** Do not mix in Week 5 or Week 8 numbers. The Week 9 baseline is the Act 3 starting point — 7 dimensions, 0–140. The Week 12 actual is the Act 3 close on the same 7-dimension instrument. Students can compare their Act 2 WIN (Week 8) to their Act 3 WIN (Week 12) separately — they are different instruments.
